## Topic summaries (top keywords)

### Topic 0 — Enterprise Software & SaaS

# What it captures:
Startups building enterprise software solutions, including SaaS platforms, cloud-based management tools, and mobile enterprise applications.

# Typical business model:
B2B SaaS with subscription-based recurring revenue; often focused on productivity, operations, or workflow optimization.

# Why it matters for VC:
Enterprise SaaS remains one of the most attractive venture categories due to predictable revenue, scalability, and strong exit potential via acquisitions or IPOs.

# Signals / risks to watch:
- Highly competitive market with crowded feature sets
- Differentiation depends on distribution, integrations, or vertical focus

### Topic 1 — Biotech & Pharmaceuticals

# What it captures:
Biotechnology, pharmaceuticals, medical devices, and health science startups focused on treatment, diagnostics, and therapeutic innovation.

# Typical business model:
R&D-heavy ventures with long development cycles; revenue via licensing, partnerships, or acquisitions by large pharma players.

# Why it matters for VC:
High barriers to entry and strong defensibility can lead to outsized returns, especially in successful clinical or regulatory milestones.

# Signals / risks to watch:
- Regulatory approval risk
- Capital-intensive with long time-to-market
- Binary outcomes tied to clinical success

### Topic 2 — E-Commerce & Marketplaces

# What it captures:
Consumer-facing commerce startups including online retail, fashion platforms, social commerce, and digital marketplaces.

# Typical business model:
Transaction-based marketplaces or direct-to-consumer (D2C) models; revenue from commissions, margins, or advertising.

# Why it matters for VC:
E-commerce enables rapid scaling and global reach, especially when combined with social and mobile distribution channels.

# Signals / risks to watch:
- Thin margins and high customer acquisition costs
- Strong competition and low switching costs

### Topic 3 — Web Infrastructure & Online Services

# What it captures:
Startups focused on web development, hosting, internet infrastructure, curated web platforms, and digital media services.

# Typical business model:
B2B infrastructure services or B2C web platforms; revenue via subscriptions, hosting fees, or advertising.

# Why it matters for VC:
These companies form the foundational layer of the internet economy and often benefit from ecosystem lock-in and scale effects.

# Signals / risks to watch:
- Commoditization of infrastructure services
- Price pressure from large cloud providers

### Topic 4 — Mobile Payments & Apps

# What it captures:
Mobile-first startups including payment solutions, mobile commerce, app ecosystems, and smartphone-based services.

# Typical business model:
Transaction fees, freemium apps, or in-app purchases; often B2C or SMB-focused.

# Why it matters for VC:
Mobile platforms enable massive user reach and rapid adoption, particularly in emerging markets and consumer fintech.

# Signals / risks to watch:
- Platform dependency (Android/iOS policies)
- Security, fraud, and regulatory compliance

### Topic 5 — Digital Health & Wellness

# What it captures:
Health, wellness, fitness, and care-focused startups using technology to improve health outcomes or healthcare delivery.

# Typical business model:
B2C wellness apps, B2B healthcare software, or hybrid models involving insurers and providers.

# Why it matters for VC:
Growing global focus on preventative care and digital health creates strong long-term demand and innovation opportunities.

# Signals / risks to watch:
- Data privacy and regulatory constraints
- Difficulty integrating into existing healthcare systems

### Topic 6 — CleanTech & Energy

# What it captures:
Clean technology startups working on renewable energy, solar solutions, energy efficiency, and sustainability technologies.

# Typical business model:
Hardware-software hybrids, infrastructure projects, or enterprise energy solutions; revenue via long-term contracts or government incentives.

# Why it matters for VC:
Climate change and energy transition are major global tailwinds, supported by policy, regulation, and public funding.

# Signals / risks to watch:
- Capital intensity and long deployment cycles
- Dependency on subsidies or regulatory frameworks

### Topic 7 — Gaming, Media & Entertainment

# What it captures:
Video games, mobile games, social entertainment, digital media, and content-driven platforms.

# Typical business model:
In-app purchases, subscriptions, advertising, or content licensing.

# Why it matters for VC:
Entertainment platforms can scale quickly with strong network effects and global consumer appeal.

# Signals / risks to watch:
- Highly hit-driven outcomes
- User retention and monetization volatility

### Topic 8 — Hardware & Consumer Electronics

# What it captures:
Hardware-centric startups in electronics, manufacturing, consumer devices, and hardware-software integrated products.

# Typical business model:
Product sales combined with software or service layers; revenue tied to manufacturing and supply chains.

# Why it matters for VC:
Successful hardware startups can build defensible ecosystems, particularly when combined with proprietary software or data.

# Signals / risks to watch:
- Supply chain complexity
- Lower margins compared to pure software plays

### Topic 9 — AdTech, Marketing & Social Platforms

# What it captures:
Advertising technology, social networks, analytics platforms, and enterprise marketing software.

# Typical business model:
Advertising revenue, SaaS subscriptions, or data-driven analytics services.

# Why it matters for VC:
As businesses digitize marketing spend, data-driven targeting and analytics remain high-value growth areas.

# Signals / risks to watch:
- Platform dependency (Google, Meta, etc.)
- Privacy regulation impacting data access
